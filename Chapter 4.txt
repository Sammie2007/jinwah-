The silence behind the obsidian doors wasn’t the absence of sound; it was the presence of something so vast that sound died in its wake.
As the doors ground shut behind Jinwah, the Bone-Chorale of the outer city vanished. He was left in a chamber of absolute, velvety darkness, broken only by a single, vertical sliver of violet light that seemed to fall from infinity.
Jinwah felt like an ant standing on the edge of a black hole. His throat was a desert of dry fear. Every instinct he’d honed over twenty years of "humane" living screamed at him to turn, to claw at the stone doors, to run back into the acidic rain of the shelters.
Then, she moved.
She didn't descend from a throne. She didn't appear in a flash of light. She simply shifted within the shadows, a silhouette that seemed to be woven from the very fabric of the night. Her skin was the color of starlight on ice, and her hair flowed behind her like a river of ink, defying gravity.
The Empress of Death.
She stood before him, and the weight of her gaze felt like physical pressure on his chest. Jinwah’s knees buckled. He didn't fall to worship; he fell because his body forgot how to support its own weight under the gravity of her history. This was the woman who had walked beside DÈÆ.TH when the world was young. She had seen the first god break.
"You are trembling," she said. Her voice wasn't loud, but it resonated in the hollows of his bones.
"I'm... I'm scared," Jinwah whispered, the truth stripped from him by the sheer honesty of the room.
"Good," she murmured, stepping into the sliver of violet light. Her eyes were not emerald like Vesper’s; they were twin eclipses—perfect circles of void surrounded by a thin, burning ring of white fire. "Fear is the only thing that keeps the humane from becoming the monstrous. DÈÆ.TH lost his fear. That was his first mistake."
She reached out, her fingers hovering inches from his cheek. Jinwah felt a pulse of cold energy leap from her skin to his. His internal Void strain surged, the nebula skin flickering across his jawline in a desperate, involuntary reflex.
"I have dreamed of your heartbeat for two decades, Jinwah," she said. "I have watched the threads of time fray around the moment of your birth. Usually, the path of a soul is a single, iron rail. For a hybrid, a glitch in the system, I expected to see two. A fork in the road."
She pulled her hand back, and the violet light in the room began to churn, projecting images onto the darkness like a spectral cinema.
"Look," she commanded.
Jinwah saw a vision of himself clad in white and gold. He stood atop a spire in the High Cities, his eyes glowing with a sterile, blinding radiance. Beneath him, thousands of hybrids marched in perfect, lobotomized unison. "The Path of Light," the Empress whispered. "You become the brood-stallion of the gods. A gilded cage where your humanity is surgically removed, and your power is harvested to create a new, controlled order. You live forever, but you never breathe again."
The image shifted, dissolving into smoke and blood. Jinwah saw himself again, but this time his form was monstrous. He was a titan of shifting nebulae, treading over the ruins of Shelter-7. The stars above were winking out, one by one. "The Path of the Void," she said, her voice heavy with ancient grief. "You become the second coming of DÈÆ.TH. You finish the war by finishing the world. You find the peace of the grave, and you take everyone with you."
Jinwah felt a sob catch in his throat. The horror of both futures was a physical weight. "I don't want either. I just wanted to fix converters. I just wanted to be... just me."
The Empress leaned in closer, her eclipse-eyes searching his.
"That is why the threads are broken, Jinwah. For the first time in an eternity, I saw a third."
The spectral light flickered. It didn't form a clear image. It was a glitch—a static of violet and human-flesh tones, a blur of motion that defied the Empress’s prophetic sight.
"The Third Path," she breathed, and for the first time, Jinwah heard something like wonder in her voice. "It is unclear. It is a mystery even to me. It is a path where the hybrid does not choose the Light or the Void, but creates something entirely new. A path where the 'sweetness' you cling to becomes a weapon, not a weakness."
She straightened, the violet light vanishing, leaving them in the heavy dark once more.
"The choice is yours, Jinwah Voidheart. I will give you sanctuary. I will give you teachers like Vesper and Khaos. But I cannot give you your destiny. You are the only thing in this universe that is truly, terrifyingly free."
Jinwah looked at his hands. In the dark, the circuits of dark energy were still glowing faintly, a map of power he didn't ask for. He was terrified—more than he had ever been in the market—but beneath the fear, a small, stubborn ember of the "technician" stirred.
He didn't know how to be a god or a monster. But he knew how to fix things.
"What happens if I fail?" he asked.
The Empress turned away, her silhouette merging back into the shadows. "Then the Third Path closes, and the world chooses for you. And trust me, Jinwah... the world is not as humane as you are."
End of Chapter Four
